# About OnlineJourno

**OnlineJourno** is an editorial-intelligence platform for newsrooms. It monitors your sources, reads how stories are being framed, and delivers desk-ready briefs — so your reporters spend their time on judgement, not on watching feeds.

A platform **by journalists, for journalists**.

## What it does

- **Source monitoring** — watches the outlets, wires and signals that matter to your beats, and surfaces what's moving now.
- **Framing analysis** — classifies how a story is being told (the PEJ frame families) so you can see the angle, not just the topic.
- **Briefs & calendar** — turns signals into a planning spine: what's ahead, what's at risk, what's ready to file.
- **Where you stand** — competitive, market-aware context: how your coverage compares with the field on a topic.

## The ethic

> The machine surfaces. The journalist decides. It never publishes.

OnlineJourno is a tool for editorial judgement, not a replacement for it. Nothing is published automatically. Every suggestion is explained — the *why* and its implications — so the desk stays in control.

## Open and self-hostable

OnlineJourno is built to be **run by the newsroom that uses it**. Newsrooms can self-host the platform, configure their own sources, signals and tools, and own their data. The wider community is invited to extend it.
