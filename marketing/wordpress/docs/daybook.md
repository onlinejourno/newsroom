# Daybook docs

> The predictive editorial calendar — see the story coming while there's still
> time to report it.

## What it is

Daybook reads the forward record — promises, scheduled events, deadlines, and
lead-times — and lays them on a calendar so the desk can commit reporting before
the news breaks, not after. It also tracks accountability: who promised what, and
whether it came due.

## Who it's for

Desk editors and planning reporters who want to be early instead of reactive.

## How to use it

1. Open [daybook.onlinejourno.com](https://daybook.onlinejourno.com/).
2. **Forward Calendar** — the Gantt of what's coming, by beat and horizon.
3. Filter by **beat** and **horizon** (30/60/90/180 days); set a
   **minimum confidence** to cut noise.
4. **Event Feed** and **Past-due** — the accountability spine: promises made,
   promises due.

## Reading it

Each item shows who promised it, the lead-time, and a confidence score. The
horizon and confidence controls are yours to tune to the desk's appetite.
