## RBI holds repo at 5.25%, lifts inflation forecast as West Asia crisis clouds outlook
**The Reserve Bank kept the repo rate unchanged at 5.25% for the second consecutive meeting, maintaining a neutral stance while raising the FY27 inflation forecast to 5.1% and cutting GDP estimates amid West Asia geopolitical stress.**

Governor Malhotra's MPC delivered no surprises on rates, leaving the benchmark at 5.25%, but the guidance mattered more: inflation is now seen at 5.1% for FY27—with a volatile path peaking at 5.9% in Q3—as energy price risks from the US-Iran conflict bite. The committee also trimmed FY27 growth projections. The decision sets the tone for bond yields, banking stocks, and the rupee through the quarter. Markets had largely priced in the hold, but the upward revision to inflation and dovish growth outlook will recalibrate rate-cut expectations.

## Centre scraps FII capital gains tax on G-secs to shore up bond flows, rupee
**The government abolished capital gains tax on government bonds for foreign institutional investors, a major policy shift aimed at attracting overseas inflows as the rupee trades near record lows.**

The rupee jumped 50 paise on the tax abolition, a direct bid to reverse FII outflows and ease pressure on the currency, which has been hammered by higher energy prices and equity exits amid the Middle East conflict. The move should tighten bond spreads and support inclusion in global indices, though efficacy depends on whether foreign investors see durable value or merely a tactical trade. Reporters should watch 10-year yield action and FII flow data in coming sessions.

## SEBI alleges 97–99% revenue inflation at Rajesh Exports; stock locked in lower circuit
**The Securities and Exchange Board of India has issued an interim order against Rajesh Exports and its promoter, alleging the company inflated 97–99% of reported revenue, triggering a two-day price rout.**

The regulator found prima facie evidence of extensive financial irregularities and has barred promoter Rajesh Mehta from dealing in securities pending investigation. Rajesh Exports denies the charges and says it is confident of resolving the issues; no fine has been imposed yet. What elevates this beyond a run-of-the-mill enforcement action is the scale—nearly ₹770 crore of LIC and retail money is at stake, with the shareholder base swelling from 22,000 in March 2020 to nearly 199,000 today. Reporters must track the adjudication timeline and any knock-on effect on small-cap sentiment or institutional redemptions.
