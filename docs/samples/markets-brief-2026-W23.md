## RBI MPC holds repo rate at 5.25%, revises FY27 inflation and growth forecasts
**The RBI Monetary Policy Committee kept the repo rate unchanged at 5.25% with a neutral stance but revised FY27 inflation upward to 5.1% and cut GDP estimates, signaling a more cautious outlook amid West Asia conflict headwinds.**

The RBI MPC's June decision to hold rates steady while raising the full-year inflation forecast to 5.1%—with Q3 at 5.9%, dangerously close to the 6% tolerance ceiling—represents a material hawkish shift that will force markets to reprice rate-cut expectations. The downward GDP revision compounds the challenge, coming as the US-Iran conflict threatens growth and inflation dynamics through higher energy prices. Governor Malhotra's forward guidance on the comfort from foodgrain stocks and the quarterly inflation trajectory (4.2% Q1, 5.1% Q2, 5.4% Q4) will determine bond and equity positioning today. Markets must parse whether the neutral stance holds or if the Committee is preparing to shift hawkish if inflation remains elevated.

## Government scraps capital gains tax on government bonds for FIIs, rupee jumps 50 paise
**In a major policy move to attract foreign flows, the government has eliminated capital gains tax on government bonds for FIIs, triggering an immediate 50-paise rupee rally from record lows.**

The tax exemption marks a significant liberalization of India's debt market regime at a moment when the rupee has been under pressure from higher energy prices and equity outflows tied to the Middle East conflict. The immediate 50-paise currency move confirms material market impact. Reporters should track whether this triggers sustained FII debt inflows, how it affects the government's borrowing costs, and whether further reforms follow to deepen foreign participation in Indian fixed income. This is a tier-1 story with cross-asset implications for forex, bonds, and potentially equities if it signals broader policy activism on capital flows.

## SEBI alleges 97-99% revenue inflation at Rajesh Exports, shares hit consecutive lower circuits
**SEBI's interim order alleging that 97-99% of Rajesh Exports' revenue over five years may have been inflated has locked the stock in lower circuits for two consecutive sessions, putting ₹770 crore of retail and LIC money at risk.**

The regulator's prima facie findings of extensive financial irregularities and possible revenue overstatement at Rajesh Exports represent a major enforcement action with immediate contagion risk. The company has barred promoter Rajesh Mehta from dealing in securities pending investigation, though no fine or penalty has yet been imposed. With 198,796 shareholders—including 194,110 retail investors and marquee institutional holder LIC—the enforcement action has triggered spillover selling in LIC shares as well. Reporters must track whether SEBI expands the investigation, how the company's clarification holds up under scrutiny, the status of trading suspensions, and whether this signals a broader crackdown on small-cap accounting quality. The sharp increase in shareholder count from 22,472 in March 2020 to nearly 200,000 today raises questions about retail exposure to potentially misleading financials.

## Foreign parents exploit India's IPO boom for repatriation as Kuku files for ₹3,500-crore listing
**Global firms are using secondary IPO exits of Indian units to repatriate billions amid high valuations, while audio platform Kuku has confidentially filed for a ₹3,500-crore offering at ₹15,000-crore valuation.**

The trend of foreign parents listing Indian subsidiaries primarily for secondary exits—not fresh capital—is driving significant outflows that weigh on the rupee, a material consequence for equity capital markets and forex reporters. Kuku Technologies' confidential DRHP submission for a ₹3,000-crore primary raise signals continued IPO momentum in consumer tech despite broader market volatility; the company is nearing operational break-even with surging revenue and plans to deploy funds in AI and content. Separately, new-economy listing Meesho is in its eighth consecutive session of decline, down 16.6% ahead of a June 9 lock-in expiry that could trigger further selling from early investors. Reporters should monitor whether officials move to curb secondary-driven outflows and track Kuku's full draft and pricing when it emerges.

## Asian markets tumble on AI-rally cooling and geopolitical risk; India money-market turnover hits record
**South Korea's Kospi plunged 5.7% and Japan's Nikkei fell 1.1% on tech selloff and US-Iran war jitters, while India's money-market turnover surged to a record as state banks borrowed heavily to fund credit growth.**

The steep regional losses—led by technology stocks—provide critical context for Indian market opens and will likely weigh on domestic IT and tech names; the geopolitical overhang from the West Asia conflict continues to pressure sentiment and foreign flows. Domestically, record money-market turnover driven by state-bank borrowing to meet booming credit demand signals potential system liquidity tightness that could have near-term rate or policy implications, especially in light of the RBI's unchanged repo rate and neutral stance. Reporters should watch for any RBI liquidity operations or commentary on credit growth sustainability.
